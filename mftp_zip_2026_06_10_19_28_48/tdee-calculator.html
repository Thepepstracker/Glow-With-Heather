💡 Understanding Your Numbers

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): The calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep your heart beating, lungs breathing, and cells functioning. Think of it as your body's "idle" calorie burn.
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): Your BMR multiplied by your activity level. This is your true daily calorie burn — the number you use to set your nutrition targets.
For weight loss: Eat 250–500 calories below your TDEE for steady, sustainable fat loss (0.5–1 lb/week). Avoid aggressive deficits which can trigger muscle loss.
Peptides and metabolism: Peptides like MOTS-C, Tirzepatide, and Retatrutide can influence metabolic rate, appetite, and fat oxidation — meaning your effective TDEE may shift over time during a research protocol.
