📋 How to Reconstitute a Peptide Vial

  1. 1
    Gather supplies: Your peptide vial, bacteriostatic water (BAC water), insulin syringes, alcohol swabs, and a clean surface.
  2. 2
    Swab both stoppers with an alcohol swab — the top of the peptide vial and the top of your BAC water vial. Let dry for 30 seconds.
  3. 3
    Draw BAC water into your syringe (the amount you entered above — typically 1–2 mL). Inject it slowly into the peptide vial by aiming the needle at the glass side wall, not directly onto the powder.
  4. 4
    Do NOT shake the vial. Gently swirl or roll it between your fingers until the powder is fully dissolved. This may take 30–60 seconds.
  5. 5
    Store correctly: Reconstituted peptides should be stored in the refrigerator (35–46°F / 2–8°C). Most last 4–6 weeks refrigerated. Keep away from light.
  6. 6
    To inject: Swab the vial stopper, draw the exact units calculated above, swab your injection site (abdomen or thigh are common), pinch skin, inject subcutaneously at a 45° angle, and dispose of needle safely.
⚠️ Important: This calculator is for educational and research purposes only. Always consult a licensed healthcare professional before beginning any peptide protocol. Dosing varies significantly by peptide type, individual factors, and research goals.
